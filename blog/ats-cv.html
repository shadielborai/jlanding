What Is an ATS CV and Why It Matters for Your Job Search

Published: September 17, 2025 Author: Jobesta Team 3 min read

Understanding ATS and ATS CVs

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software designed to help employers streamline the recruitment process. Instead of manually reviewing hundreds of applications, recruiters rely on ATS to automatically collect, filter, and rank candidates based on keywords, qualifications, and experience.

An ATS CV (or ATS-friendly CV) is a resume structured in a way that can be correctly read and interpreted by these systems. Unlike highly designed CVs with graphics, columns, or nonstandard formatting, an ATS CV uses a simple layout, clear section headings, and optimized keywords to ensure the information is accurately parsed and evaluated.

The Challenges of a Non-ATS CV

When a CV is not ATS-friendly, candidates face two significant obstacles.

First, there is the extra manual effort required. Many systems will be unable to automatically extract education, work history, training, and certifications from a poorly formatted CV. This forces applicants to enter every detail manually into online application forms, turning a simple submission into a tedious, repetitive process—particularly for candidates applying to multiple positions.

Second, there is the risk of missing critical information. Even with manual adjustments, a non-ATS CV can still cause important qualifications or experiences to be overlooked. If the system cannot interpret certain details, the candidate may be screened out before a recruiter ever sees the application.

These issues are especially important given how widespread ATS usage has become. Recent industry surveys show that around 98% of Fortune 500 companies use an ATS in their hiring process, and overall, about 75% of companies across industries rely on ATS technology to manage recruitment. Adoption is especially high among larger organizations: nearly nine out of ten companies with more than 500 employees use an ATS or HRIS system. Smaller organizations are catching up too, with more than a third of companies under 100 employees using such systems.

The implications are clear: if your CV is not ATS-compatible, you may be excluded from consideration by the vast majority of employers. In fact, research indicates that up to 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS filters before they ever reach a human recruiter.

The Advantages of an ATS-Friendly CV

Creating an ATS CV eliminates these risks and provides several advantages:

  • Efficiency: Your CV transfers smoothly into online systems without requiring you to re-enter information manually.
  • Completeness: All sections—experience, education, skills, and training—are captured and presented accurately.
  • Visibility: Your application is more likely to pass the automated filters and reach a recruiter’s desk.
  • Relevance: By aligning with job description keywords, your CV matches what employers are actively searching for.
  • Professionalism: A structured, easy-to-read CV creates a stronger impression for both software and human reviewers.

With so many employers depending on ATS technology, having an ATS-friendly CV is no longer optional—it is essential for standing out in today’s competitive job market.
