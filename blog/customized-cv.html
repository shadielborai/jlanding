Why Customizing Your CV for Each Job Makes All the Difference

Published: September 16, 2025 Author: Jobesta Team 3 min read

In today’s job market, sending out one generic CV to every position is no longer enough. Recruiters receive hundreds of applications for many jobs—and they spend very little time per CV. Customizing your CV for each job isn’t just a “nice to have,” it can make or break your chances.

How Much Time Does a Recruiter Spend Reviewing Your CV?

The numbers are eye-opening:

  • On average, recruiters spend just 5-7 seconds in an initial scan of a CV. (The Undercover Recruiter)
  • In similar studies, 6 seconds is repeatedly cited as the typical time to decide whether a CV will move further in the process. (Cambridge Network)
  • Some reports show recruiters conducting a 30-second review at most before discarding or advancing a resume. (KSL.com)
  • Many job adverts receive on average 250+ applications, meaning that even a few seconds per CV quickly adds up. (Purshology)

These statistics highlight that recruiters often make first impressions very quickly. If your CV doesn’t immediately look like it fits the job, it may be rejected almost instantly.

First Impressions Matter — Relevance Is Key

Because recruiters only have seconds, how your CV appears at first glance plays a huge role. If a CV seems generic, irrelevant, or not tailored to the position, it sends several negative signals:

  • The candidate hasn’t taken time to understand the role.
  • The candidate’s skills or experiences may not match the job’s needs.
  • It suggests carelessness or lack of interest.

Relevant job titles, recent experience, key skills, and keywords from the job description must be clear at first glance. If these aren’t obvious, the CV often ends up discarded.

How Customization Improves Your Chances

Customizing your CV per job helps you in multiple ways:

  1. Highlight relevant experience and skills first so they’re obvious in the recruiter’s quick scan.
  2. Use keywords from the job description to align with both ATS systems and recruiter expectations.
  3. De-emphasize irrelevant information to keep focus on what matters most.
  4. Format for readability with clear headings, consistent layout, and concise bullet points.
  5. Show interest and effort, signaling you care about this job, not just any job.

The Risk of a Generic CV

Statistics confirm that a one-size-fits-all approach puts candidates at a disadvantage:

  • 75% or more of CVs are rejected or ignored early in the process, often for lack of relevance or keyword match. (Cardinal Staffing)
  • Of the 250 resumes typically submitted for a corporate job, only 4-6 candidates are invited to interview. (Purshology)
  • Less than 5% of recruiters spend more than a minute on a CV that doesn’t immediately appear relevant. (Cardinal Staffing)
